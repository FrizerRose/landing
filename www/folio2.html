About me

I've migrated into Front-End in 2016, leaving 5+ years of hobby and freelance work in graphic, web design, 3d modeling and animation.

Over the last three years I worked with many standard technologies and tools, and some that are not well known or adopted but are amazing.

These have become an irreplaceable part of my workflow. I've been using Photoshop, Illustrator, Bootstrap, jQuery, PHP, Wordpress, ACF, Twig, CSS, SCSS, VanillaJS, GSAP, ScrollMagic, AnimeJS, VueJS, Nuxt, etc.

I have been using bundles/task-runners like Gulp, ParcelJS and Webpack to create and configure starters/boilerplate code.

To organize CSS I have been using BEM and was very impressed at first, but later, after looking at other methodologies, ended up using or trying out ITCSS, OOCSS, SMACS, InuitCSS and TailwindCSS.

What turned out best was a version of ITCSS but with a shorter BEM syntax. It introduces a well thought-out folder structure as well as a way to categorize CSS classes.

I love what I do. Always looking for opportunities to work with talented and inspiring people, who are more experienced and know more than me.

When starting a new project it is very important for me to have a clear path all the way to the finish. Learning new things is can be fun, but only if it is done at a manageable pace.

The tool needs to fit me, not the other way round.

Listed below are principles that are important for me.

The Good

  • Well organized, systematic approach
  • Work hard the first time, then improve and tweak with every new iteration
  • Don't repeat yourself (DRY code)
  • KISS (Keep it super simple)
  • Low specificity in CSS (Flat class selectors)
  • Clear separation of concerns
  • Humility

The Bad

  • Poor leadership
  • Sloppiness and negligence in the workplace/codebase
  • Don't start what you cannot finish
  • Manipulating people
  • Low specificity in CSS (Flat class selectors)
  • Clear separation between front and back-end code
